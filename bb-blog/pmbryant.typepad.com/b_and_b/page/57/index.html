A good sign

In our local paper today, the San Antonio Express-News, there is an op-ed by a regular contributor, who always seems to adopt the party-line GOP voice in his writings: J. Francis Gardner. His attempt at spinning the economy in this article is a good example of how pathetic and self-contradictory the GOP's arguments are this election year (emphasis added):

Presidential election campaigns fan the flames of folly by suggesting that whoever sits in the Oval Office will influence the U.S. economy. Baloney.

The president of the United States couldn't sabotage a healthy economy if he wanted to, and he can't jump-start an ailing economy no matter what he does.

Presidents don't tax or spend â€” Congress does. The president can suggest until he's blue in the face, but it is the legislature that controls the purse strings. Alan Greenspan, in his role as Federal Reserve chairman, has more impact on the economy than any president.

In money matters, presidents get credit when situations brighten and get blamed when circumstances tank. It's a quaint and well-entrenched notion, but intellectually lazy.

Intellectually lazy, huh? Mr. Gardner's favorite powerless President appears to feel differently.

From remarks by George W. Bush at a rally in Traverse City, Michigan today (emphasis added):

We've overcome these [economic] obstacles because of the hard work of America's entrepreneurs and farmers and ranchers and small business owners and workers. We've come through a lot. We've also come through it because of two well- timed tax cuts. (Applause.)

We didn't pick winners or losers, we did it the fair way: If you pay tax, you get relief. (Applause.) We helped our families by raising the child credit. We reduced the marriage penalty. I mean, what kind of tax code is it that penalizes marriage? We ought to be encouraging marriage in America. (Applause.) We helped the small businesses and, this time, the check actually was in the mail. (Laughter.) Because we acted, our economy since last summer has grown as a rate as fast as any in nearly 20 years. Because we acted, Americans added about 1.5 million new jobs since last August. (Applause.) The national unemployment rate is down to 5.5 percent.

GOP spin, thankfully, but probably not surprisingly, is growing more pathetic by the day.

AP goes one-sided on us

Just my luck, I was thinking as I was poking around the net examining news stories today. I finally start a blog, and the first day out the media (at least the online print media) isn't doing such a bad job. Most of the stories I checked out today were pretty straight. Some presented spin points, but at least did so evenhandedly, including the spin points of both sides. Lazy, but fair.

But predictably, I finally came across an example of blatantly one-sided spin being passed along in a supposedly straight news story. From the AP via ABC News: Bush, Kerry Press for Women's Votes:

There may be no greater commodity in presidential politics than a voter like Burnosky an undecided woman with no firm party allegiance. "I'd like to vote against Bush," she says, "but I don't know whether Kerry would keep us safe."

Polls show that single women generally favor Democrats, married women lean Republican, elderly women are more Democratic. Bush and Kerry are targeting swing-voting women such as Burnosky who defy categorization with their independence.


Standing at her computer, EMILY's List political director Karen M. White taps on the keyboard and pulls up the Bush re-election Web site. She clicks on the icon linking her to the president's new television ad. EMILY's List is a political action committee that helps pro-abortion rights Democratic women.

The faces of Bush and his wife, Laura, fill the computer screen. "I can't imagine the great agony of a mom or a dad having to make the decision about which child to pick up first on Sept. 11," the president says, promising to take the fight to terrorists before they strike again.

"That's a good spot," White murmurs. "Good spot. They're trying to appeal to swing-voting women, trying to remind them that they live in dangerous times. If they live in fear, they may choose to stick with the status quo," she says.

After a pause, she adds: "Smart woman will look at that ad and say, 'Yes, great. But what have you done to make us safer?'"


And a bit later:

Laura Bush's appearance in the new TV ad is no mere detail. Democrats say she's an effective surrogate in the pitch to women voters.

Tricky, tricky. Most of the times Dems are quoted or referred to in this article, they are praising the Bushies' approach. Ugh. Alas, this is a technique I've seen way too often since I started paying close attention to political coverage, back during the 2000 campaign.

Here are the people quoted in this article: two undecided voters, who both apparently voted for Bush in 2000; one of these gets in some pretty harsh comments on Kerry that Karl Rove could have said himself; George Bush in one of his ads; Laura Bush; and White of EMILY's List. The only left-leaning, possible Democratic voice is that of White, and the quotes selected from her are, as I said above, rather friendly to the other side.

Extremely one-sided, and sadly, not at all unusual.

August 15, 2004

A Beginning

At long last, we are on the verge of re-claiming our country from the abuses of the current Republican administration. Victory is in sight, but clearly a lot needs to happen between now and early November to ensure that this victory actually happens. And preferably in a strong, decisive fashion, that will make any future administration think very hard about acting the same way again. I hope that by starting this blog, amongst other things, I can help in my own small way to make this happen.

One of my primary objectives is to use this blog to spread awareness to family and friends and anyone else who happens to find their way here about how the popular media passes along GOP spin points, misrepresentations, and sometimes even outright fabrications about the Kerry-Bush campaign. Not necessarily because they are biased, but often just because they are lazy. One of my inspirations is Bob Somerby of The Daily Howler, a site which I encourage everybody who has any interest in politics and the media to read regularly, if you don't do so already. I suspect I'll be pointing out his writings rather regularly here.

Doing something like this has been on my mind for several months, but the final inspiration came a few days ago when reading a post on Democratic Underground made by a strategist for the Kerry campaign. He wrote:


John Kerry's chief opponent is NOT George Bush. It's the media. And you can help defeat Bush by doing everything in your power to fight media spin.

One of the ways to do it is to help promote and support the kinds of websites that Paul Krugman featured in a New York Times op-ed last week: Media Matters, Daily Howler, Campaign Desk, and so on.

Another is to join forces here and form your own media watchdog group.

Another is to join John Kerry's Media Corps.

Another is to start your own website listing media transgressions and linking to other such sites. (Form a webring.)

Another is to come up with creative ideas about how to counter media spin and sharing those ideas here.

This really struck home with me. Living in Texas, not anyone's idea of a swing state, I've (mistakenly?) not been inspired to get involved in on-the-ground campaigning so far. But this is something I can help with, that we all can help with no matter where we live. I've already signed up for Kerry's Media Corps, and I hope you do too.

So please let me know your thoughts on the campaign so far. What are you hearing from the media about the campaign? What are you hearing from your friends and acqaintances about this Presidential campaign? What else are you doing to help ensure the defeat of George W. Bush in November?

Thanks for listening, and please check back regularly.
